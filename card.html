American Bison

Bison are the largest animal in North America. They are also fast, running up to 35 MPH. They can jump high fences, and are strong swimmers.

  • Scientific Name: Bison bison
  • Average Length: 6.6-11.5 feet
  • Average Life Span: 12 to 20 years
  • Habitat: Open grasslands in N. America

The front and back of the American bison have different shades of brown fur. It protects the bison from the rough elements of the plains, spanning Mexico to Canada. They are often referred to as Buffalo, which are Asian. They can reach up to 11 and a half feet in length and are fast and agile, despite their lumbering appearance. They fertilize the soil as they thrust their hooves into the soil.
